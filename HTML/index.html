Abdul Sattar Edhi


I admire greatly is Abdul Sattar Edhi, a humanitarian and philanthropist
who dedicated his life to serving humanity. He is often referred to as the
"Angel of Mercy" and is the founder of the Edhi Foundation, one of Pakistan's
largest and most respected welfare organizations.


Admirable Qualities About the Legend

☛ Edhi's compassion for the underprivileged and marginalized is unparalleled. He believed in serving humanity without discrimination, reaching out to people of all backgrounds, religions, and ethnicities. His empathy for others, especially those in dire situations, motivated him to establish the Edhi Foundation, which provides a wide range of services including orphanages, shelters for the homeless, free healthcare, and ambulance services.

☛ Starting with very limited resources, Edhi's journey was not without challenges. He faced resistance, financial difficulties, and at times, opposition from various quarters. However, his unwavering determination and resilience allowed him to build a vast network of services that have saved countless lives and provided hope to millions.

☛ Throughout his life, Edhi lived modestly, dedicating all his resources and energy to helping others. He famously wore simple clothing and lived in a small room adjacent to his organization's headquarters. His selflessness is evident in how he used donations to expand services rather than for personal gain, setting a powerful example of living for others.

☛ Edhi had a clear vision of a society where everyone had access to basic necessities regardless of their socio-economic status. His leadership in establishing and expanding the Edhi Foundation demonstrates his ability to turn this vision into reality. Under his guidance, the Foundation grew into a nationwide network, known for its efficiency, transparency, and integrity.

☛ Despite his global recognition and numerous awards, Edhi remained a humble man. He never sought personal accolades and often credited his success to the generosity of the Pakistani people and his dedicated team. His humility made him even more beloved, as he remained focused on the cause rather than the accolades.
